You have written your manuscript and want to know if it is worth anything? First of all, yes! You wrote a flippin' book! And now what do you do? Well it just so happens we have put the next steps in a simple format for you. So go and get that edit on! but first...

 

**1. Celebrate**

 

Celebrating what you have completed so far can seem like an unnecessary step. But, don't skip this one. If we don't send our sub-conscious this kind of signal, it holds onto stress. This can build over time and hinders your creativity.

 

You don't need a lot of time or money to get the full benefits of celebrating. Some of our favorite ways to do this part is acknowledging our progress by sitting back, throwing on a big smile and allow yourself to do something you don't always have time for. This can be a goal written on a special bottle you get to open when you finish, or a new class you want to explore, even as simple as relaxing in a hammock.... so nice and we so often don't make time for in our busy world.

 

**2. Pause**

 

To the casual observer, this may appear like you are not doing anything at all. But how wrong they are! This part of the editing process allows all the pieces that have been hovering just beneath your conscious mind to coalesce. It also provides your brain a small time frame to reset and get more insights to the written content versus the vast, imagined story in your head.

 

This is not the same thing as losing momentum. So don't confuse laziness with pausing. Make sure to set a set period of time to give your brain space to make connections for you, to settle aspects of irritation, and answer questions that have been quietly gnawing at you. Two weeks can be a moderate time frame.

 

Enhance this step with meditation, nature walks, and mindless projects. Do not turn on a podcast or consume media while you are doing these things. Silence is your ally here! 

 

 

 

**3. Read it like a reader**

 

Now is the most iconically editing step, open your manuscript and take notes! Don't necessarily read it on your computer in your office. Allow a different part of your mind to activate. Read your work as if you didn't know the detailed backstories of the characters and you weren't sure how it was going to end.

 

"The secret to editing is simple. You need to become its reader not its writer." -Zadie Smith

 

Ask yourself what promises your making to the audience and make sure the end answers the important ones. Make sure it is easy for your audience to follow your story. Do not be afraid to cut out what isn't working. If it isn't supporting your story it is killing it. If you wrote it beautifully, pat yourself on the back, know that you can write more beautiful prose and let it go.

 

"Writing is easy. All you have to do is cross out the wrong words."

\- Mark Twain.

 

 

Make sure to double-check any research or location information. Do your homework, this may be a lot of work, but it pays off!

 

 

**4. Implement.**

 

Ok, this step is so simple and oh, so difficult. This is where you go through and make all the changes you took notes on and show your story's truth. This does feel daunting, but try to enjoy the process. Get in a creative and exploratory mindset. Look only at one piece at a time, so the whole manuscript doesn't overwhelm you. See, simple.

 

**5. Send it to a few ALPHA readers**

 

Having another person read your manuscript is invaluable! Not all of your trusted people will enjoy your genre, that's great! Use them anyway. They can see missing parts clearly and spot promises that have not been met. They can tell you what parts are boring or they lost interest and if your characters are connecting.

 

"Write with the door closed, rewrite with the door open."

\- Stephen King

 

Who you choose as your readers will make a big impact. While it is ego boosting to get all positive feedback, "like you are so amazing!" By the way, you are amazing and it feels good. But this won't elevate your novel or show you areas you can improve. (If you happen to have one reader that is all about pumping you up, I'll allow it.)

 

Prepare yourself to accept feedback and disconnect your value with what they say. Allow your mind to see this for what it is, an opportunity. It is also a form of love, these are readers who have taken time to really work with you, appreciate it!

 

Also, if you are comfortable enough with your process or have a system with your readers you can easily switch steps 3 and 5.

 

**6. Implement**

 

This time is not where you simply apply all the edits. You have been given suggestions, so use them as such! If your readers do not understand a part or don't like it, the immediate answer is not to cut (though it may be). Sit with the why behind the feedback and play with options. Find a solution that supports your story the best, even if it isn't how the reader would choose. Sometimes it may even be best to ignore the edit opinion. Do not sacrifice your vision or your voice. Let the suggestions help you see what you feel needs to be done with your story.

 

 

**7. Send it to BETA readers**

 

Not to repeat myself... but I want you to! Your novel is better then it has ever been. Get excited! Send it to the audience that you are writing for, they can show you more specific fixes they might appreciate. This time you can be a little more confident in what you are sending out to more readers. Choose people who are safe and still will push you. This is time to get as much feedback as you think you can take, because after this, you are sending off your manuscript to a professional.

 

**8. Implement**



You know the drill, another round! Do not expect this time to be easier, it may be, but if it isn't don't let that surprise you. Look over all the feedback, check what is consistent from all readers and prioritize those. Make sure you stay true to your story and your intuition. Do not merely accept all feedback! Allow yourself to let go of scenes that aren't translating, change directions that make sense, and accept that you have room to improve but you are still killin' it!

 

Finally! Your manuscript is ready for professionals! Whether your goal is a traditional publish or a self publish using a professional will polish your manuscript. You will have a more refined message and be able to showcase to a broader audience. Find an editor or agent that you vibe with. If you enjoy their style they are more likely to get you where you want to go. Not everyone will see your vision, do not let rejections (from agents or readers) get to you; they are inevitable if your saying anything that actually stands for anything. Ok, now go celebrate, Again!
